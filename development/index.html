JAN 4TH 2026 HELLO, WORLD, AGAIN

It's been too long.
to say that "try to get a job in an industry related to what i went to school for" went poorly would be, ah...
an understatement.
I was unemployed for 7 months, during which time some bad stuff happened, I discovered why everything's been so hard for me since I turned 21 (I have OCD! Yayyy (sound of loud explosion)), and I played a little game called Great God Grove.
Sidenote: everyone reading this should play Great God Grove. To say it, and the friendships I've made while speedrunning it (oh, hey, I'm a WR speedrunner now - eventually I'll add that to the about me :) ) helped keep me alive in 2025 would not be an exaggeration.
I eventually got a job in a printing center, which, while not directly related to game development, was employment, so 'woohoo' - except. Um have you ever seen an episode of Kitchen Nightmares where it's a family run establishment, and there's a husband and wife team who are constantly screaming at each other, and the staff, and there's raw chicken next to cooked chicken? Yeah, that's what it was like. I quit that job after two months of trying to grin and bear gettin yelled at (do NOT try and grin and bear that kind of workplace, reader, your sanity is not worth the paycheck), gave up the last of my dignity, and applied for a retail petstore job.
guys i kind of love retail petstore job.
like ok it has nothing to do with my schooling and i would rather move into the woods and learn how to use linux and blender than work a full shift as cashier, but I have so much experience with keeping and working with aquariums / zoo exhibits that I am really, really good at the petcare side of the job, and it's fulfilling ! even when i get yelled at for not selling someone a goldfish for a 5 gallon!!! (i would. like to stop getting yelled at but its so much better than the last place oh my god)
during all of this, in December, I realized something. I realized that the OCD thoughts that spiraled out of the AI bubble (may it pop this year, please) was what was keeping me from even thinking about Warp Zone. I realized that I was signifigantly more depressed than I thought I was, and I had neglected my artistic passions for a year and a half - over two, if we consider the starviewer the last major addition to this project. It was kind of upsetting to consider how long it's been. I wanted to have so much more done by the time my birthday rolled around this year, but there's no use in beating myself up about it.
here's whats going to happen.
I'm going to slowly add some more of the art i've made to the art-corner, I'm going to add a bit about speedrunning in the about-me, i'm going to finally get the damn shrimp server up and running again once I move all my little guys to their new fancy 16gallon home, and, maybe, just maybe, I'll make progress in getting a bit closer to my actual vision for what Warp Zone should be.
Personal life goals : move out of this goddamn house before it kills me, convince retail job to let me be fulltime, or trick an aquarium into paying me money.
I hope you're all doing well, sincerely. I'm sorry for just disappearing. I see so many beautiful messages in my guestbook that boil down to "this is cool, its a shame im late / its over" and it just... hits home for me, a little. ADHD is a disease of perpetual disappointment. You can have lots of lofty goals and ideas and passions, and then you can blink, and now you're having to worry about getting health insurance. That gap between the ideas you have and the failure of your body to make them, the feeling that you will die unfinished... It's kind of what Warp Zone's supposed to be about, if i can ever get to the state where i make it.
that sounded more intense than i meant it to, im generally doing a lot better than i was last year at this time. again, got a diagnosis! that means actually able to figure out why i was feeling the way i was, and working on it! i still have bad weeks, but i can THINK again, and it's amazing. so, uh, i won't be making any promises on work on this site. I won't tell you i'd like to have something done by X amount of time. All i'll say is...
There's some stuff i'd like you to see one day.

    MAY 06 2022 MADE MY OWN GUESTBOOK

    So, yesterday (5/5/22), suddenly out of nowhere, my original guestbook that was hosted on freegb.net just... vanished. along with the entire site. Accoding to wikipedia, a 410 error means that whoever was running the site deleted it deliberately to make my life miserable, so that's awesome! OTL.... essentially, i've lost everyone's very nice messages. This led me on a spite-filled quest to make my own guestbook. Sadness @sadgrl.online has a great tutorial that tells you how to do this with PHP and CPanel, but i hate paying money for things, so i decided to take a trip down the path of darkness and evil: making a guestbook with Google Firebase.
    This should be easy. by all accounts, it really, really should. hell, there's even a tutorial for using firebase that walks you through, step-by-step, making a guestbook! Fun, right?
    no. web development was a mistake. everything is terribly designed and no one wants you to use their tools.
    i spent like 6 hours on a voice call with real programmers frank and peter (hi, guys!) who both had tried doing firebase integration before, and my god it was a nightmare. just... look, if you want to do it, i encourage you to look at the page source and just try and swap out values. seriously, steal my code, because i am literally incapable of understanding it now that its written. i will be completely unable to help you if it doesnt work. it's a miracle that its functioning at all.
    dramaticism aside, if you'd be so inclined to leave a new message on the guestlog, i'd appreciate that - they're a really great motivational tool for me. :')
